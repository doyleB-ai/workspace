## 1. TARGET ALLOCATION RATIONALE

### Equity/Bond Split: 85/15 (including REITs + Gold)

**Why 85% equity at age 36 with a 19-year horizon:**

- Classical "120 minus age" rule suggests 84% equity — we're right on target
- Client has demonstrated high risk tolerance (willing to hold 35% in one stock)
- $400K household income provides large ongoing contributions that act as a "bond-like" human capital asset
- 19 years is long enough to recover from even severe drawdowns (2008 recovery took ~5.5 years)
- Home equity ($500K) provides additional non-correlated wealth

**Why not 100% equity:**

- Two young children create near-term cash flow needs (childcare, potential home expenses)
- Early retirement at 55 means the accumulation window is shorter than typical
- 8% bonds + 7% alternatives = enough dry powder to rebalance into equities during crashes
- The 8% bond allocation acts more as "rebalancing ammunition" than income generation

### Geographic Split: 60% US / 25% International

**Why 25% international (vs. current ~1%):**

The current portfolio has essentially zero international diversification. This is the single biggest gap.

- US represents ~60% of global market cap — 60% US allocation matches market weight
- International stocks (especially developed ex-US and EM) are trading at ~30% valuation discount to US
- CAPE ratio: US ~35x vs. International Developed ~18x vs. EM ~13x
- Currency diversification matters when you earn, spend, and invest all in USD
- Over rolling 10-year periods, international has outperformed US roughly 40% of the time
- The last decade of US dominance is partially a valuation expansion story that may not repeat

**The international allocation:**
- 15% Developed (VXUS or VEA) — Europe, Japan, Australia, Canada
- 5% Int'l Small Cap Value (AVDV) — factor premium in less-efficient int'l markets
- 5% Emerging Markets (VWO or AVES) — China, India, Brazil, Taiwan, etc.

### Factor Tilts: Small Cap Value

**Why 10% US SCV + 5% International SCV:**

The Fama-French size and value factors have delivered a combined premium of ~3-5% annually over US large cap growth over long periods. While not guaranteed, the economic rationale is sound:

- Small companies have higher cost of capital → higher expected returns
- Value stocks (low price/book, low price/earnings) are cheaper because they're riskier → risk premium
- AVUV (0.25% ER) has outperformed VTI by ~4%/year since inception (Sept 2019)
- AVDV (0.36% ER) has outperformed VXUS by ~5%/year since inception (Sept 2019)
- These are Avantis (sub-brand of American Century) funds that use a systematic, rules-based approach

**Key point:** SCV has higher volatility and can underperform for extended periods. This is why it goes in the Roth and HSA — long time horizon + tax-free growth maximizes the benefit.

### Key Takeaway from the Math

**The Recommended portfolio has a HIGHER expected return AND LOWER volatility than the current portfolio.** This is the core insight — diversification is the only free lunch in investing.

- **Current portfolio Sharpe: 0.18** — terrible risk-adjusted return due to single-stock concentration

**Translation:** By diversifying, you're likely to end up with MORE money AND sleep better at night. The concentrated portfolio only wins in the very best scenarios (90th+ percentile), and even then only marginally.
