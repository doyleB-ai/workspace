## 3. SEGMENT ANALYSIS

### AWS ($128.7B revenue, 24% YoY growth, 35.4% operating margin)

**The crown jewel.** AWS re-accelerated from a ~13% growth trough in 2023 to 24% in Q4 2025 — its fastest growth in 13 quarters. Key metrics:

- **Revenue run rate:** $142B annualized (Q4)
- **Backlog:** $244B, up 40% YoY, 22% QoQ — this is contractually committed future revenue
- **Operating income:** ~$45.5B in FY2025
- **Market share:** ~28-32% of global cloud infrastructure (still #1, but Azure is closing)
- **AI revenue:** Multi-billion-dollar run rate, growing triple digits

**Jassy's $600B AWS target:** On a recent call, Jassy stated AWS could reach $600B in annual revenue by 2036, implying ~17% CAGR from the current $142B run rate. This is ambitious but not unreasonable given the $244B backlog and AI tailwinds.

**Key concern:** AWS growth is the linchpin. If it decelerates below 20%, the entire $200B capex narrative falls apart. Q1 2026 earnings (expected late April) will be the first checkpoint — analysts are watching for AWS revenue above $37B to confirm the 24% growth rate didn't peak in Q4.

### Advertising (~$68B revenue, ~22% growth)

**The under-appreciated gem.** Amazon's advertising business has grown from essentially zero a decade ago to a ~$68B run rate, making it the third-largest digital ad platform behind Google and Meta. Q4 2025 advertising revenue was $21.3B, up 23% YoY.

Why this matters: Advertising revenue is nearly pure profit. It leverages Amazon's existing traffic and purchase-intent data. Every dollar of ad revenue flows almost directly to operating income. This business alone is worth $300-500B on a standalone basis at peer multiples.

**Growth drivers:** Prime Video ads (launched 2024), Thursday Night Football, sponsored product enhancements, and Rufus AI shopping assistant creating new ad surfaces.

### North America Retail (~$399B revenue, ~12% growth)

The logistics revolution is real:

- **Regionalization:** 8 self-sufficient fulfillment regions have dramatically cut delivery times and costs
- **Robotics:** 1M+ robots deployed (Proteus autonomous bots, Sequoia for picking, Blue Jay for sorting)
- **Same-day delivery:** Leadership position with fulfillment centers increasingly close to population centers
- **Operating margin:** ~7% in Q4 2025, up from near-zero in 2022

**Amazon Haul:** Launched late 2025 to compete with Temu/Shein on ultra-low-cost imports (items under $20). Early signal that Amazon won't cede the low end.

### International (~$143B revenue, ~10% growth)

After years of losses, International turned consistently profitable in 2025 by replicating the NA regionalization model. Still a drag on overall margins but trending in the right direction.

## 4. THE $200B AI BET

### What Amazon Is Spending On

The $200B capex plan breaks down approximately as:

1. **AWS data centers & AI infrastructure:** ~70-75% ($140-150B)
   - 1M+ NVIDIA GPUs (Blackwell, Rubin architectures)
   - Custom Trainium2 (1.4M chips deployed) and Trainium3 (launching mid-2026)
   - Data center construction globally
2. **Logistics & fulfillment automation:** ~15-20% ($30-40B)
   - Robotics expansion, delivery infrastructure
3. **Project Kuiper/Amazon Leo satellite:** ~5% ($10B)
4. **Other (devices, content, etc.):** ~5% ($10B)

### The Trainium Silicon Strategy

This is Amazon's most ambitious hardware bet:

- **Trainium2:** 1.4M chips deployed in fastest-ever ramp. Used for majority of Bedrock inference.
- **Trainium3:** 40% better price-performance vs. Trainium2. Expected near-full commitment by mid-2026.
- **Trainium4:** Arriving 2027 with further performance jumps.
- **Key advantage:** Custom silicon allows Amazon to offer lower-cost AI compute vs. NVIDIA-dependent competitors.
- **Key risk:** Two senior Annapurna Labs executives have departed in recent months (Gadi Hutt, Rami Sinno), raising leadership continuity concerns.

### The OpenAI Deal

In early 2026, Amazon invested $50B in OpenAI as part of a larger funding round. OpenAI committed to $100B in AWS spending over 8 years and 2 GW of Trainium chip usage. This is massive validation for both AWS and the custom silicon strategy — but it also means Amazon is paying to acquire its own customer.

### When Will AI Capex Pay Off? Historical Precedent

**AWS Investment-to-Profitability Timeline:**
- AWS launched: 2006
- First profitable year: 2015 (~9 years)
- Revenue at profitability: ~$8B annually
- Cumulative capex 2006-2014: ~$30-40B

**AI Investment Timeline (projected):**
- AI capex ramp began: 2023
- Cumulative AI-related capex 2023-2026: ~$400-500B
- AI revenue run rate (Q4 2025): ~$15-20B (estimated)
- Break-even on AI capex: **2028-2029** (if precedent holds)
- Full profitability contribution: **2030+**

**Critical difference:** The AI capex is 10x the scale of early AWS. The historical parallel gives comfort that Amazon knows how to invest ahead of demand, but the magnitude of the bet means the consequences of being wrong are proportionally larger.

**Leading indicators to watch:**
1. AWS revenue growth rate (must stay >20%)
2. Trainium utilization rates vs. NVIDIA instances
3. Bedrock enterprise customer count and deal sizes
4. AWS backlog growth trajectory (>30% YoY)
5. Capex-to-revenue ratio beginning to decline (target: <20% by 2028)

**My assessment:** The AI bet will likely pay off, but not on the accelerated timeline bulls are pricing in. Expect 2028 as the earliest year where AI capex begins generating meaningful net-positive FCF. The period 2026-2027 will be the maximum pain point for shareholders.

## 9. EMPLOYEE-SPECIFIC CONSIDERATIONS

### RSU Vesting Schedule

Amazon RSUs typically vest on a back-loaded schedule:
- Year 1: 5%
- Year 2: 15%
- Year 3: 40%
- Year 4: 40%

**Key implication:** If the client is in years 3-4 of a grant, the majority of value vests in the near term. This creates a natural "sell as it vests" opportunity. If in years 1-2, the back-loading means most value is at risk for an extended period.

**Recommendation:** Sell RSU shares as they vest, unless there's a specific tax reason to hold. The ongoing grants provide continuous new AMZN exposure — there's no need to accumulate.

### ESPP Strategy

Amazon's ESPP typically offers a 15% discount on the lower of start-of-period or end-of-period price.

**Recommendation:** Continue participating in ESPP (it's essentially free money), but **sell ESPP shares immediately upon purchase.** The 15% discount is the guaranteed return. Holding adds concentration risk for uncertain additional upside.

If shares are held <1 year after purchase (or <2 years from offering period start), gains are taxed as ordinary income. If held longer, qualifying disposition gives more favorable tax treatment. However, the tax benefit of holding for qualifying disposition must be weighed against concentration risk. In most cases, selling immediately is the right call.

### Tax Implications of Selling

- **RSUs at vesting:** Taxed as ordinary income at vesting. Selling immediately has minimal additional tax impact (short-term gain/loss only on any price change since vesting).
- **ESPP shares:** Disqualifying disposition (sold <1yr after purchase) means discount is ordinary income. May be acceptable given concentration risk reduction benefit.
- **Long-held shares:** If original shares have significant gains, consider:
  - Spreading sales across tax years
  - Tax-loss harvesting elsewhere in portfolio to offset
  - Charitable giving of appreciated shares
  - Using losses from any underwater lots to offset gains

### Amazon Employee-Specific Risks

1. **Layoff correlation:** Amazon has done multiple rounds of layoffs. Job loss + stock decline = double hit.
2. **RTO policy:** Amazon's 5-day return-to-office mandate has created attrition. Forced relocation or departure means RSU forfeiture.
3. **Non-compete/clawback:** Verify whether any agreements impact selling strategy.
4. **Insider trading windows:** Employee may have blackout periods around earnings. Plan sales during open windows.
